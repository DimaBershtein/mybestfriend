Hi, everyone! My name is Sol, and my adoptive father's name is Dima (he is the one who built this website for your convenience)

In 2015 it was me (the dog with the white fur and In those days my name was Solomon) connected to an iron chain 24/7 for the sake of guarding a factory. Thanks to one of the posts on Facebook, Dima and his wife Natali saved me from a miserable life and even from death. Dima built this site with the intention of helping as many dogs as possible find a warm and loving home like he gave me. In Israel there are thousands of dogs that are in associations and kennels just waiting for someone to come and adopt them. On this website you will find links to the websites of all kinds of amazing associations. Those associations that save the lives of homeless dogs and cats, they are looking for a new family for them that will give them a second chance at a happy life. I hope that through this site many dogs will find new families who can give them a warm and loving home and in return the family will discover what true unconditional love is In addition, the same mothers manage to save dogs mainly thanks to the donations of wonderful people. The money from the donations goes to food, medicine, vaccinations and care for the dogs. All your donation will give the associations to save more dogs and find them a warm home that will take care of them and love them.

And here it is 7 years since I was adopted - I am with my beloved family.

Why is it better to adopt a mixed breed dog?

It should be noted that the vast majority of dogs in associations are mixed dogs. Many people when looking for a dog would prefer to adopt a purebred dog for all kinds of reasons. Now...don't get me wrong, purebred dogs are amazing dogs and there are good reasons to adopt a purebred dog. But here are some of the advantages that you may not have known about why it is better to adopt a mixed dog

Reason Number One

Every year in Israel about one hundred thousand dogs are killed because they have not found a home for them. As you can imagine, we are not talking about purebred dogs, but mixed dogs, from unwanted or random litters or those that arrived in another way in the pens of animal cruelty and various associations. The best solution to this situation is first of all the adoption of dogs from public kennels, when you gain a great, healthy and vaccinated dog, and the dog - which otherwise might be a candidate for euthanasia - gains a loving family and a warm home.

SOS For Animals

The S.O.S Hayut association was established in 1980 by Ricky Batsari, Dr. Ze'ev Sheiner and Mona Halfman, with the aim of helping homeless dogs and cats and improving the condition of animals in Israel. Riki Batsari, who still serves as the volunteer chairman of the association, is the living spirit behind the association's activities throughout the years.

YAD4 - The national database for animal adoption

Yad 4 is a unique project and the first of its kind for cooperation between the associations and the municipal quarantines working to rescue abandoned animals in Israel. Today, over 65 non-profit organizations, activists and closures are members of the website and the arm is still tilted. The search engine on the site includes dogs of all breeds, ages and colors that are candidates for adoption throughout the country.

Animal cruelty Ramat Gan

Ramat Gan and Vicinity was founded in 1944 by Mrs. Greta Biran. Since the society’s establishment, its members have cared for hundreds of abandoned and neglected animals, providing for their medical care and daily welfare while searching to find them loving, adoptive homes.

Roof For Animals

The Rooftop Association for Living was founded in May 1989 by an amazing woman named the late Ruth Waltshansky. Ruth had a vision to build a shelter for abandoned animals in Holon. We, the successors of her path, did everything to realize the vision and in 2012 we opened the most beautiful and well-invested shelter in Israel.

Klavlove

The kennel is operated by the Dog Love association that rescues and cares for abandoned dogs, rehabilitates them, takes care of housing, food, treatments and vaccinations and looks for a good and suitable permanent home for each of them.

Animal cruelty Rehovot

The association began its first steps in Rehovot back in the seventies and was officially established in 1984. The association is an independent association, which exists solely from the income of the veterinary clinic and donations. Independent of or connected to any other animal cruelty association.

Home Dog

The kennel volunteers work in the municipal quarantine, which takes in stray and abandoned dogs from all over Tel Aviv. The activity focuses on daily walks, improving the well-being of the dogs in the kennels, advertising the dogs and finding adoptive homes.
